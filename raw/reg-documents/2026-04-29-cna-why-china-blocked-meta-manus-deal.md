# Why China Blocked the Meta-Manus Deal

> Source: https://www.channelnewsasia.com/east-asia/china-block-meta-manus-deal-reasons-sino-us-ai-tech-rivalry-6087941
> Collected: 2026-05-11
> Published: 2026-04-29

Channel NewsAsia reports that China blocked Meta's acquisition of AI startup Manus even though the deal was structured through a Singapore-registered entity after Manus shifted headquarters there.

The article frames the case as evidence that Beijing is willing to extend control over technology it still sees as strategically Chinese even after offshore relocation. Analysts quoted in the piece argue that regulators are looking through formal incorporation structures toward the underlying substance of the company: where the technology was developed, where the data originated, where the founding team remains anchored, and whether the capability is strategically sensitive.

The article connects the case to intensifying Sino-U.S. competition over AI and to a broader shift in Chinese policy toward greater control over cross-border technology, talent, and data transfers. It suggests that offshore hubs such as Singapore may remain useful for capital raising and international headquarters structures, but no longer serve as reliable shields if the company remains Chinese in substance.

The article also suggests the decision is intended to send a signal to other firms that China will not tolerate the transfer of strategically important AI capabilities to U.S. buyers merely because a legal entity has moved offshore.
