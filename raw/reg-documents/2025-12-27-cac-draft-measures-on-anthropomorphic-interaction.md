# CAC Draft Measures on Anthropomorphic Interactive AI Services

> Source: https://www.cac.gov.cn/2025-12/27/c_1768571207311996.htm
> Collected: 2026-05-11
> Published: 2025-12-27

The Cyberspace Administration of China published a notice soliciting public comment on the Draft Interim Measures for the Administration of Humanized Interactive Services Based on AI.

The draft applies to AI products or services provided to the public in China that simulate human personality traits, thinking patterns, and communication styles and interact emotionally through text, images, audio, video, or similar media.

The state says it combines healthy development with governance according to law, encourages innovation, and applies inclusive and prudent, classified and graded supervision to prevent abuse and loss of control.

The draft prohibits content and interactions that endanger national security, disrupt social order, manipulate users emotionally, damage mental health, encourage self-harm, induce unreasonable decisions, or extract sensitive information.

Providers are assigned primary responsibility for safety and controllability across the full lifecycle, including algorithm review, ethics review, information review, cybersecurity, data security, personal information protection, anti-fraud measures, major risk planning, emergency response, and suitable content management personnel and tools.

The draft also requires mental health protection, emotional boundary guidance, dependency risk warning, training-data management, user-state recognition, emergency intervention, minor mode, elderly protections, interaction-data protections, explicit disclosure that users are interacting with AI, time-based reminders, and easy exit from emotional companionship services.

It adds annual and event-triggered security assessment requirements, platform review duties for app stores, algorithm filing requirements, sandbox-style security service platform construction, and enforcement powers including warnings, rectification orders, service suspension, and interviews with responsible executives.
