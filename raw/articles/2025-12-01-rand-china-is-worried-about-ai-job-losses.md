# China Is Worried About AI Job Losses

> Source: https://www.rand.org/pubs/commentary/2025/12/china-is-worried-about-ai-job-losses.html
> Collected: 2026-05-11
> Published: 2025-12-01

This RAND commentary argues that Beijing sees no contradiction between trying to win in AI and managing the social risks of AI-driven labor disruption. The article places China's AI diffusion strategy inside an already fragile labor market marked by youth unemployment, pressure on low-skilled migrant labor, shrinking white-collar opportunity, and broader economic slowdown.

The commentary argues that the CCP may accept some short-term job losses as a price of progress, but it also sees labor-market instability, depressed consumption, welfare strain, and possible social unrest as serious political risks. It points to signals that Beijing may manage the pace and shape of AI adoption through employment-oriented interventions such as tax incentives, wage subsidies, reskilling programs, and potential limits on displacement in some job categories.

The source is useful because it adds a labor-stability layer to the China corpus: AI is not only an engine of productivity and national power but also a source of social risk that Beijing may actively modulate rather than unleash without constraint.
