# How Might the United States Engage with China on AI Security Without Diffusing Technology?

> Source: https://www.rand.org/pubs/commentary/2025/01/how-might-the-united-states-engage-with-china-on-ai.html
> Collected: 2026-05-11
> Published: 2025-01-30

This RAND commentary argues that the United States may need some engagement with China on AI safety and governance because transnational AI risks can affect U.S. security regardless of where systems are built. At the same time, it warns that technical safety cooperation can be dual use and may improve Chinese AI capabilities.

The article suggests a safer path is nontechnical engagement: risk management frameworks, incident reporting, red-line thresholds, lessons from safety institutes, and other governance topics that build relationships and trust without diffusing sensitive technical know-how. It draws an analogy to Cold War cooperation on permissive action links while emphasizing that many AI safety techniques can also improve model capability.

This source matters because it gives the U.S. knowledge base a clearer middle position between total non-engagement and capability-sharing cooperation.
