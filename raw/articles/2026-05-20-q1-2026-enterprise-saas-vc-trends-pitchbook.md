### **AI mega-rounds drive historic enterprise SaaS funding quarter**

Enterprise SaaS VC activity surged in Q1 2026, despite the public-market narrative that AI would compress software valuations and threaten the SaaS model. Deal value reached a record $173 billion across 867 deals, driven by OpenAI’s $122 billion raise and xAI’s $20 billion raise, but the quarter still looked healthy even when excluding those two outliers.

Q1 showed that enterprise SaaS is not disappearing. SaaS is instead being rewritten around AI-native workflows, infrastructure-heavy platforms, and more defensible systems of record. Major fundraises from Wayve, Cloover, Advanced Machine Intelligence, Kraken Technologies, and Luma AI highlighted investor appetite across autonomous systems, energy management, AI infrastructure, vertical SaaS, and multimodal AI.

*VC deal value skyrocketed in Q1 as AI fueled software investment, especially OpenAI ($122 billion) & xAI ($20 billion). Exits were similarly distorted by the $250 billion acquisition of xAI.*
