Nvidia, Palantir and CoreWeave are all AI stocks but offer potential investors very different potential rewards and risks. Getty The AI boom kicked off in May 2023. That’s when AI chip designer Nvidia announced a surprising leap in demand for its graphics processing units – convincing me that the rapid growth in ChatGPT users was evidence of the next big thing. While Nvidia leads the pack of publicly-traded AI companies, CoreWeave, which went public in March 2025 by monetizing its stash of GPUs, has also benefited from the growth in spending on AI data centers which train and operate AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Gemini and Claude. Palantir, which uses data analysis to help some government agencies and private companies get their jobs done, is different from Nvidia and CoreWeave because it uses AI chatbots, but does not invent and deploy its own. Nvidia, CoreWeave And Palantir At A Glance Although Nvidia, CoreWeave and Palantira are leaders in their respective industries, they offer potential investors very different potential rewards and risks. The Case For Nvidia (NVDA) Nvidia, whose shares have risen 79% in the year ending May 12, is the leader in AI chip design. It competes primarily in the hardware accelerator and semiconductor market , providing the GPUs (like the H100, B200 Blackwell, and Rubin architectures) that AI data centers use to train and operate chatbots. Nvidia’s share of the AI GPU market is around 90%. It wins and keeps customers through competitive advantages , including its proprietary CUDA software platform (into which developers are functionally locked), InfiniBand networking and rapid pace of new product introduction which rivals cannot match. MORE FOR YOU The Case For CoreWeave (CRWV) CoreWeave, shares of which have soared about 82% in the year ending May 12, turned a cache of Nvidia GPUs it was using for bitcoin mining into a gold mine as a provider of AI cloud services. As a so-called neocloud provider, CoreWeave competes directly in the specialized AI cloud infrastructure market , providing GPU-as-a-Service for AI training and inference. It wins by being faster and more specialized than general-purpose hyperscalers like AWS. Through a partnership with Nvidia, which has invested in CoreWeave, the neocloud receives priority allocations of Nvidia’s B200 and /H200 chips. CoreWeave’s other advantages include specialized liquid cooling and rapid deployment of new computing capacity. The Case For Palantir (PLTR) Palantir, shares of which have risen about 13% in the last year ending May 12, provides software and data analytics to government agencies and private companies. It competes in the enterprise AI software, data integration and defense analytics markets – offering an Artificial Intelligence platform. Palantir’s strength is its near-monopoly in U.S. defense data integration. It wins commercial and government customers via its proprietary ontology, which maps disparate data sources such social media posts and geolocation data, high-level security clearances and its AIP Bootcamps. The Side-By-Side Comparison Of NVDA, CRWV And PLTR Stock While Nvidia is the most financially solid of the three companies, CoreWeave’s stock has appreciated the most in the last year. Meanwhile Palantir is a relatively poor performer in the stock market with considerable losses. Key Metrics To Understand Readers should understand how well the company has performed relative to investor expectations. For AI stocks , investors focus on whether the company’s revenue growth and margins are exceeding expectations and whether their guidance for the future is better than they had anticipated. Investors also look at whether the company’s backlog is increasing faster than the consensus outlook and the strength of their balance sheets. Growth Outlook Comparison Based on these metrics, Nvidia seems to have the most compelling outlook for its operations. Nvidia (NVDA) Growth Outlook Due to strong demand from hyperscaler and sovereign AI developers, Nvidia’s outlook remains optimistic, with Wall Street projecting Q1 FY2027 revenues to hit $78.8 billion , a 79% increase compared to the previous year. However, the company faces headwinds including fabrication capacity from its partner TSMC and geopolitical export restrictions targeting China. CoreWeave (CRWV) Growth Outlook CoreWeave’s revenue growth is also rapid – assisted by a 65% increase in global AI inference demand . The primary headwinds for CoreWeave are heavy capital expenditure requirements to build out data centers and the risk of AI startup funding drying up. Palantir (PLTR) Growth Outlook Management expects a 61% increase in 2026 revenues to roughly $7.19 billion , This growth is fueled by a projected 115% growth in U.S. commercial adoption. Tailwinds include heavy defense spending and enterprise AI integration. A high dependency on government contracts, intense competition, extreme valuation pressures and reputational challenges could impede Palantir’s growth. Analyst Opinions Analysts are most bullish about Nvidia – offering a moderate buy for CoreWeave and a hold rating for Palantir. What Analysts Say About Nvidia (NVDA) Analysts are overwhelmingly bullish, with approximately 95% rating the stock a &quot;Strong Buy.” Nvidia&#x27;s analyst sentiment matters to investors because the company’s trajectory is a proxy for the AI boom. If analysts are optimistic about Nvidia, the entire tech sector benefits. What Analysts Say About CoreWeave (CRWV) The street consensus on CoreWeave is a &quot;Moderate Buy&quot; with price targets around $127. Analysts see the company as a structural player for the next decade . Investors should care because CoreWeave’s health is an early indicator of the health or lack thereof of the private AI startup sector and whether specialized clouds will take market share from hyperscalers. What Analysts Say About Palantir (PLTR) Analysts are most cautious about Palantir. They frequently issue Hold or Underweight ratings. Investors should care about this skepticism because the primary narrative on Wall Street is multiple compression, meaning the stock’s high price-earnings ratio makes the company vulnerable to any failure to beat expectations and raise guidance each quarter. Risks To Consider The three companies face distinct business risks: Nvidia’s physical and geopolitical risk. The AI chip designer’s success relies heavily on global hardware supply chains (TSMC) and navigating complex U.S.-China export controls. CoreWeave’s financial and ecosystem. The neocloud’s survival is tied to the health of the broader AI startup ecosystem and Nvidia&#x27;s continued hardware dominance. If Nvidia falters, or if startup capital dries up, CoreWeave&#x27;s heavy debt burden becomes dangerous. Palantir’s valuation and software competition. Unlike hardware, nimble competitors can undermine software moats. Palantir is battling the expectation of flawless execution; at a 154x P/E multiple, they must grow earnings very rapidly just to justify the company’s current price. Risks To Consider Before Investing In Nvidia (NVDA) Beyond geopolitics, Nvidia&#x27;s biggest risk is its own customers. Tech giants like Amazon, Google and Microsoft are pouring billions into developing custom silicon (ASICs) to reduce their heavy reliance on Nvidia&#x27;s expensive hardware. Risks To Consider Before Investing In CoreWeave (CRWV) Severe concentration risk and leverage are other key risks investors should consider. CoreWeave relies on the Nvidia hardware ecosystem, and it carries a heavy debt-to-equity ratio of 4.46 from building out its infrastructure. Risks To Consider Before Investing In Palantir (PLTR) Palantir&#x27;s multiple is priced vastly higher than its software peers. If U.S. commercial growth decelerates even slightly, the stock risks severe compression. Is Nvidia, CoreWeave Or Palantir The Better AI Stock Buy? Before considering an investment in these stocks, investors should consider the company’s ability to sustain very rapid revenue and profit growth. Investors should seek answers to questions such as which company is best able to compete in the fastest growth market segment, win and sustain market share dominance, earn high operating margins, finance its growth without going into too much debt and maintain a rapid pace of innovation. Nvidia, CoreWeave and Palantir are all fast-growing companies. Of the three, Nvidia has demonstrated the ability to sustain rapid growth, high margins and a pace of innovation that outcompete rivals. If Nvidia can sustain expectations-beating growth in the future, the stock may continue to rise.
