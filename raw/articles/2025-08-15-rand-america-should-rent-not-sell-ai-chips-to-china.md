# America Should Rent, Not Sell, AI Chips to China

> Source: https://www.rand.org/pubs/commentary/2025/08/america-should-rent-not-sell-ai-chips-to-china.html
> Collected: 2026-05-11
> Published: 2025-08-15

This RAND commentary argues that the United States should provide China with access to AI compute through cloud rental rather than sell advanced chips outright. The core claim is that cloud access preserves U.S. control and leverage because access can be turned off, monitored, and renegotiated, while physical chip exports permanently transfer the asset.

The commentary argues that cloud delivery can create similar revenue benefits while making unauthorized transfer harder and enabling know-your-customer screening, including scrutiny of connections to Chinese military entities. It also argues that neither sales nor rentals will stop China's semiconductor sovereignty push, but that rentals preserve more strategic flexibility for the United States.

The source also notes that Beijing itself is using regulatory uncertainty and energy rules to preserve demand for domestic chip substitutes, suggesting that both Washington and Beijing are trying to shape the chip market as a geopolitical instrument rather than as a neutral trade domain.
