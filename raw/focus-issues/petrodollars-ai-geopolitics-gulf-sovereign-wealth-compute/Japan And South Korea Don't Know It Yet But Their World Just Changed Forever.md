Japan And South Korea Don't Know It Yet But Their World Just Changed Forever

Japan and South Korea.

They are not in the room. They were not consulted. And the decisions being made may fundamentally change their security situation in ways they cannot easily counter.

Here is why.

For 75 years Japan and South Korea have operated under a security guarantee from the United States. American military bases. American nuclear umbrella. American naval presence in the Pacific. In exchange these countries have hosted American troops have bought American weapons and have supported American foreign policy even when it conflicted with their own interests.

It was an arrangement that worked. A little uncomfortable but stable.

Now Trump is in Beijing potentially offering China a deal on Taiwan that transfers the front line of Asian security from American hands to Japanese and Korean hands.

Think about what that means practically.

If Taiwan comes under Chinese influence China gains the ability to potentially control shipping lanes that Japan and South Korea depend on for 80 to 90 percent of their energy imports. Every tank of oil every unit of liquefied natural gas that keeps Japanese factories and Korean shipyards running passes through waters that China could theoretically threaten.

Japan and South Korea suddenly need to spend massively on their own military capabilities. They need to make their own deals with China. They need to decide how independent from America they actually want to be.

Trump didn't tell them this was coming. He just changed the board.

America gets out of a 75-year security commitment it can no longer afford. China gets Taiwan. And Japan and South Korea get a problem they didn't ask for and weren't prepared for.

Do you think Japan and South Korea were betrayed? Or is this just the reality of great power politics?

Prof Jiang Xueqin
