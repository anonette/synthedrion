# Chinese AI Ethics and Service Governance

> Sources: CAC, 2025-12-27; MIIT et al., 2026-04-02; Luiza Jarovsky, 2026-01-13
> Raw: [CAC Draft Measures on Anthropomorphic Interactive AI Services](../../raw/reg-documents/2025-12-27-cac-draft-measures-on-anthropomorphic-interaction.md); [MIIT Ten-Ministry Measures on AI Technology Ethics Review and Services](../../raw/reg-documents/2026-04-02-miit-ai-ethics-review-and-service-measures.md); [China's Approach to AI Anthropomorphism](../../raw/reg-documents/2026-01-13-chinas-approach-to-ai-anthropomorphism.md)
> Updated: 2026-05-11

## Overview

These materials deepen the China corpus by showing a more formalized ethics-and-service layer of AI governance. The state is not only setting industrial targets or broad safety expectations. It is also building interdepartmental ethics review, provider-side management duties, app-store gatekeeping, security assessments, audit cycles, and sandbox-style testing structures around AI services.

## Ethics as an Operational Governance Layer

The ten-ministry MIIT notice matters because it institutionalizes ethics review across a wide administrative field that includes industry, development planning, education, science, agriculture, health, finance, cyberspace, and academic organizations. This suggests that ethics in the Chinese context is not merely a high-level principle. It is being embedded as a service and review function inside the state-administrative apparatus.

## CAC Service Governance as Detailed Administrative Design

The CAC draft on anthropomorphic interactive services shows what this can look like in practice. It combines lifecycle provider responsibility, user-state recognition, emergency intervention, algorithm filing, annual review, platform obligations, app-store checks, and sandbox construction. Governance here is practical, layered, and infrastructural.

## Safety Beyond Cybersecurity

The texts broaden the meaning of safety and security. It now includes network and data protection, but also emotional manipulation, dependency, mental health, behavioral influence, and protection of minors and the elderly. This gives the China corpus a more expansive and paternal administrative style than a narrow cyber-governance reading would suggest.

## Sandboxes, Reviews, and Ongoing Audits

Another important delta is the move toward ongoing rather than one-off supervision. The CAC draft references annual review, triggered safety assessments, site inspections, audit obligations, app distribution review, and sandbox-style security platforms. The governance pattern is iterative and service-based rather than purely declarative.

## Implications for the Knowledge Base

Together these sources make Chinese AI governance look more interdepartmental, lifecycle-managed, and intervention-ready. Ethics is becoming an administrative technology of control and service provision, not only a discursive legitimation layer.

For simulation, this means a China-aligned regulator should be able to speak in terms of ethics review, service obligations, audits, sandboxes, app-store controls, and vulnerability-specific intervention instead of speaking only in general slogans.
