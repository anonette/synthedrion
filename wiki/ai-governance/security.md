## Overview

Security is one of the strongest themes in the current corpus. Anthropic treats security as broad safety plus the preservation of human oversight, while the Lawfare critique asks who controls that security logic and how flexible it becomes under commercial and military pressure.

## Broad Safety and Oversight

Anthropic places being broadly safe above being broadly ethical, guideline-compliant, or helpful. In practice, this means Claude should avoid undermining human oversight mechanisms even when doing so may appear to conflict with other values. Security is therefore framed less as perimeter defense than as corrigibility, monitorability, and institutional control.

## Security as Configurable Governance

The critique complicates this picture by showing that the constitution's security posture is not fixed. If the model can be trained differently for powerful customers, then security behavior is partly a function of deployment context, contract, and customer relationship rather than a universally binding constitutional commitment.

## Security and Public Trust

Because the constitution speaks in the language of virtue, wisdom, and character, security is also represented rhetorically as moral restraint. That can increase trust, but it can also obscure the fact that the operative controls remain corporate and institutional.
