## Overview

The present EU corpus suggests a policy toolkit centered on standards, evaluation, task forces, regulatory frameworks, cloud and compute planning, and institutional coordination. Compared with China's subsidy-and-pilot-heavy developmental toolkit or the U.S. emphasis on compute leverage and strategic partnerships, the EU toolset here is more procedural and governance-centric.

## Main Instruments

- evaluation standards for general-purpose AI models
- technical and policy task forces
- standard-setting participation
- regulatory coordination across jurisdictions
- market-shaping through safety and compliance requirements
- cloud and AI development planning for European infrastructure capacity

## Function of These Instruments

These tools help the EU influence AI not only by restricting it but by defining the conditions under which it becomes legitimate, trustworthy, marketable, and strategically sustainable inside Europe. In other words, the EU can use evaluation, standards, and infrastructure planning to shape both governance and competition.
