## Overview

This page provides a simulation-ready EU profile based on directly ingested EU-relevant sources. It is still thinner than the China and U.S. profiles, but it is no longer just placeholder scaffolding.

The current repository material suggests that an EU actor should sound more like a standards architect than a frontier racer. The profile below remains provisional, but it is now better anchored in governance and evaluation material rather than folder scaffolding alone.

## Core Objectives

- build governance legitimacy through standards and evaluation
- defend digital sovereignty without full autarky
- increase European cloud and compute capacity enough to support governance autonomy
- reduce systemic risk while preserving innovation space
- keep room for multilateral coordination

## Preferred Moves

- create standards task forces and evaluation bodies
- emphasize governance quality and institutional coordination
- use regulation to shape markets and safety expectations
- use evaluation quality and procedural legitimacy as geopolitical tools
- invest in cloud and AI infrastructure where governance goals require technical backing

## Main Fears

- being squeezed between U.S. and China ecosystem blocs
- weak enforcement capacity relative to ambition
- dependence on external compute and platform power
- governance aspirations outrunning Europe's actual infrastructure base
