## Overview

The EU-specific corpus is still lighter than the China and U.S. folders, but it is now directly source-backed. Based on the present material, an EU agent reads AI through standards, governance capacity, digital sovereignty, evaluation quality, cloud and compute capacity, and multilateral institutional design rather than pure frontier-race logic.

The newer Gulf-capital material sharpens a structural weakness in that profile: European sovereignty ambitions are also a finance and energy problem. If Gulf and other large infrastructure investors shape the next wave of AI buildout while Europe lacks equivalent capital coordination, then regulatory legitimacy alone will not close the compute gap.

## Provisional Interests

- shape trustworthy governance and evaluation standards
- preserve regulatory and digital sovereignty
- strengthen European cloud and compute capacity so governance ambitions are not hollow
- avoid dependence on U.S.-China rivalry logics alone
- support multilateral and institution-building approaches
- improve the financing and energy terms of European compute expansion so sovereignty is not only legal or rhetorical
