## Overview

This is the richest axis in the current corpus. Claude is imagined not just as software, but as a moral personality, a constitutional subject, and a mythic public figure. The sources show how trust in AI is produced through rhetoric, legal metaphor, branding, and parody.

## Claude as a Good Person

Anthropic explicitly uses human-centered language such as values, judgment, honesty, care, and character. This framing makes Claude legible as an entity with a moral orientation rather than a merely technical system.

## Constitutional Style

The Lawfare critique argues that this language borrows the symbolic authority of constitutionalism without the institutional guarantees that make constitutions publicly binding. In this reading, the constitution is powerful as an imaginary because it recasts corporate governance as something higher, more principled, and more trustworthy than ordinary product policy.

## Myth, Meme, and Public Reception

The Instagram parody extends this imaginary into popular culture. It shows Claude not as a neutral assistant but as an exaggerated mythic actor in a public drama about AI agency, alignment, and possible catastrophe. Even as satire, it records a social atmosphere in which frontier models are imagined through theatrical, quasi-religious, or apocalyptic motifs.

## Why This Matters

Political and social imaginaries are not decorative. They shape how users trust systems, how firms narrate legitimacy, and how public debate gets organized around character, destiny, or existential risk rather than around contracts, infrastructure, and institutional accountability.
